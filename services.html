Our Core Services

Ten powerful services designed to transform your grocery shopping and meal planning

Receipt-to-Menu Setup

What it is: Snap a photo of your grocery receipt or forward an e-receipt; we parse items, normalize brands/sizes, and load them into your pantry with quantities.

Why it matters: Most people don't struggle to cook—they struggle to decide. Automating the jump from "what's in the bags?" to "here's your week" cuts the nightly stare-down with the fridge and reduces midweek store runs. Decision fatigue around "what to cook" is a top complaint in meal-planning threads.

Pantry Intelligence & Use-Up-First Logic

What it is: A living inventory that tracks freshness windows, opened items, and multi-packs; the planner schedules perishables earlier in the week and rolls leftovers forward.

Why it matters: Households waste food because items get buried, dates aren't checked, and storage isn't optimized. Use-up-first cues and "you've got two peppers left" prompts counter the most common pantry pitfalls and overbuying.

Constraint-Aware Weekly Planner

What it is: A 7-day dinner plan generated around your pantry, time, and diet—diabetes-friendly, vegetarian weekdays, high-protein, spice level, "not fishy," cilantro-averse, and more.

Why it matters: Families juggle tastes, schedules, and health goals; plans fall apart when they ignore constraints. Paper Chef keeps hard rules (allergens, diet targets) while preserving flavor, so you aren't making two separate dinners.

Instant Swaps & Pantry Reconciliation

What it is: Swap a recipe (or key ingredient) with one tap; we adjust the pantry and reschedule remaining items automatically.

Why it matters: Real life changes. When soccer practice runs late or ground turkey became ground beef, the plan adapts without breaking your inventory or blowing the budget—avoiding last-minute takeout.

Focused Top-Up Lists (Not a Re-Shop)

What it is: A short, exact add-on list for what you don't have, grouped by aisle/section with quantities.

Why it matters: The two biggest shopping complaints: repeat purchases of items you already own and vague lists that lead to impulse buys. A precise, pantry-aware list helps you buy only what the week needs.

Waste-Cutting Insights

What it is: "Use-by" alerts, leftover trackers, and a weekly "saved from waste" summary tied to your actual ingredients.

Why it matters: Food wasted at home is expensive and avoidable; small habits—planning before shopping, using leftovers intentionally, storing correctly—deliver real savings. Paper Chef nudges those habits in-app.

Family Mode & Shared Kitchens

What it is: Shared pantries, assign-a-cook by day, and simple links so everyone knows "what's for dinner" and where leftovers live.

Why it matters: Households often lose meals to miscommunication ("I thought that was for tonight" / "I gave it away"). Light coordination features protect the plan and your prep time.

Prep Timeline & "Tonight at a Glance"

What it is: A Today card with cook time, steps, and any quick prep (thaw/soak), plus "tomorrow" hints.

Why it matters: People abandon plans when the day's task is unclear or too complex. A single glance reduces mental load and helps you stick to the plan even on busy nights. Time scarcity is the #1 blocker in meal planning.

Storage & Pantry Best-Practice Prompts

What it is: Contextual tips like "keep the fridge at 35–38°F" and "store onions/garlic outside the fridge," shown only when relevant to ingredients you've bought.

Why it matters: Better storage extends ingredient life and reduces spoilage—your cilantro lasts longer, your berries don't go mushy, and your budget thanks you.

Privacy & Control

What it is: Redaction of payment/loyalty details, optional auto-delete of receipt images after parsing, and editable inventory history.

Why it matters: Your shopping data is sensitive. We keep it scoped to your kitchen, and you stay in charge.
